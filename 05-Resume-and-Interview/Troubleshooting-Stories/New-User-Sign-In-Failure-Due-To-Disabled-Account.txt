I worked on a scenario where a newly created user was unable to sign in after their account was set up in Microsoft Entra.

I started by verifying that the account existed and confirming that the username was correct. Instead of immediately resetting the password, I attempted to reproduce the issue by signing in with the user account, which failed.

Next, I checked the user’s account status in Microsoft Entra and found that the account was disabled. Since a disabled account prevents all authentication attempts, this explained why the user could not sign in.

I re-enabled the account and tested the sign-in process again. This time, the login was successful and reached the first-time password change prompt, confirming that access had been restored.

This experience reinforced the importance of checking basic account states early in the troubleshooting process before moving on to more complex steps like password resets.
